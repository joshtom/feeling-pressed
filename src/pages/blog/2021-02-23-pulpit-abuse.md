A pulpit is a podium or platform upon which preachers stand to rest his Bible and writing pad. Every preacher is expected to be equipped with pulpit etiquettewhen entering ministry work or is to minister on another pastor’s pulpit. This etiquette is a way of demonstrating respect to the pews/members of a church. A preacher’s pulpit etiquette is not only a expression on him/her, but also the church leadership as a whole.

In short, there are fowl statements a preacher should/should not say on the pulpit.

A preacher is required to say statements:

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->(1)<!--\[endif]-->Which glorify God

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->(2)<!--\[endif]-->Which edifies humanity (Ephesians 4:12)

“Let each of us please his neighbor for his good, leading to edification” (Romans 15:2).

*“Therefore I write these things being absent, lest being present I should use sharpness, according to the authority which the Lord has given me for edification and not for destruction” (2 Corinthians 13:10)*



“Therefore let us pursue the things which make for peace and the things by which one may edify another” (Romans 14:19).

“And He Himself gave some to be apostles, some prophets, some evangelists, and some pastors and teachers, for the equipping of the saints for the work of ministry, for the edifying of the body of Christ” (Ephesians 4:11-12).

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->(3)<!--\[endif]-->Which makes man have sense of eternity in their minds.

Any sermon that doesn’t encapsulate the above listed factors should not be titled *sermon.*Such should rather be called self aggrandizement.

Haven’t read what was published on Apostle Suleman by The Punch Newspaper on 18th February, 2021 (<https://punchng.com/why-i-prayed-that-covid-19-should-not-end-apostle-suleman/>), I could not believe it until I watched it. You may need to watch it to believe my point. Just click this link-- <https://youtu.be/VPJ_Z11NdMw>

The recklessness is unexpected of a learned Preacher. I think he displayed the highest level of thoughtlessness that a sermon giver could behave. He violated pulpit and ministerial ethics and such should not be overlooked as we have done in the time past.

In the said video, the cleric further stated that he was praying for the pandemic not to end because he was prospering and resting. *He said, “In COVID (pandemic) I bought a jet; the third one. I have three. I was praying for COVID-19 not to end because I was resting*. *While people were complaining, my wife asked, ‘Can life be this sweet?’*He is widely known as an apostle of fire but the pressing question is did Elijah rejoice that there was famine during his time? Or did he pray that the famine should continue? The statement is stinking. I can’t just comprehend a heaven-minded Apostle say such! It is high time Nigerian preachers exercise restraint on their pulpit way and manner. Enough of Pulpit desecration in the name of operating under divine anointing.

I’m ashamed that instead of the membership to wail for their Aggrandizer perhaps he will repent and withdraw such ugly statement, rather, they applauded him to continue driving in the broad way that lead to eternal damnation! Anyone, irrespective of popularity/influence/wealth, whose standing on a pulpit does not glorify God, edify men and influences man to have eternity at heart is defiling the essence of the Pulpit.

Who is his example? Jesus Christ expressed concern for the multitude that were hungry; my Lord *borrowed*from among *his disciples,*multiplied the food and provided surplus for the needy (Matthew 14:13-21). Also, when the first wave of COVID-19 was intense, I watched a video where Rev’d Dr. Paul Enenche and his wife (both medical Doctor) volunteered themselves to help Government attend to COVID-19 patients and donated reliefs for the needy Nigerians but Apostle Suleman bragged that he was enjoying, resting and even bought his third private jet (which worth over $500,000) while people were complaining. What did he do to those he heard complaining that the economy was bad? Nothing!

I can’t imagine what would be running in the nerves of his church members whose businesses crashed at that time! Homes went apart because of financial imbalance while some Christians engaged in illegal activities to survive the period yet their Apostle added to their sore by saying that he bought his third private jet and even prayed that such ugly moment to continue. Did he listen to himself and still feel proud to apologize God and his membership? His disciple must, by now, be ashamed of one of his disciple.

When asked if it is true that he has money printing machine at home, he said, “Somebody asked if it’s true and I said it is true. They said, ‘It’s risky o’. I said I didn’t know it’s risky because I already bought the machine. When you speak in tongues, you’re printing money.”

Simon in Acts 8:18-20 intended to buy the gift of the Holy Ghost from the apostles and he was rebuked by Apostle Peter. From where did Suleman buy his speaking in tongues? How much did he buy it? Is the seller aware of the impending danger in selling? I suppose that we now have several Simon on the pulpit. It is high they are rebuked before they shut the border of heaven at lackadaisical Christians.

I am saying with poise that speaking tongues that is gotten from God does not guarantee worldly riches, it is a coded language used to communicate with God. Whoever that is in this habit should be cautious of their unbridled ministry exercises as no offender will escape the Judgment of God.

This epistle is not to condemn his anointing but to caution his pulpit unrestrained behavior.
